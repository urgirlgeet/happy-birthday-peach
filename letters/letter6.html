My pretty flower,

It’s National Flower Day today, and somehow it feels like the perfect excuse to sit down and talk about you, because if I really think about it, loving you has always felt like walking through a garden where every flower means something different, something beautiful, something us.

You know how red roses are supposed to stand for love? The loud, undeniable, heart-racing kind of love. That’s you when you look at me like I’m the only person in the room, when you hold my hand just a little tighter for no reason, when you remind me, without even trying, that I am loved, deeply and completely. It’s the kind of love that doesn’t ask for attention, but still steals it effortlessly.

But then there are sunflowers, the kind that turn toward the light no matter what. That’s you on my dull days. The way you somehow pull me out of my overthinking spirals, the way you make everything feel a little warmer, a little brighter, just by being there. You don’t just bring light into my life, you are the light I lean toward, even on days I don’t realize I need it.

Lilies are about devotion and quiet strength, and that’s the version of you I see when you show up for me in the simplest ways. Not loud, not flashy, just steady. The kind of love that doesn’t need to prove itself because it’s always there, grounding me when I feel like I might fall apart. You don’t always say a lot, but somehow you always say enough.

And daisies, they’re soft, innocent and honest. That’s us when we’re laughing at the dumbest things, when I’m being completely unfiltered around you, when I don’t have to try to be perfect. With you, I get to just be, and that kind of comfort is rare.

Orchids will always be special to me, not just because they stand for something rare and beautiful, but because they were the first flower you ever gave me. I don’t think I’ll ever forget that moment, the way something so simple held so much meaning. Every time I think of orchids now, I don’t just think of beauty, I think of the beginning of us, of a memory I’ll always carry.

And then there are tulips, your favourite and somehow my favourite too now. I think that makes them even more meaningful to me, because loving you means loving the things that matter to you too. Tulips stand for deep, quiet love and comfort, and that’s exactly what you are in my life. You’re the kind of person I can run to when everything feels too loud, too heavy, too much. You don’t try to fix me like im broken, you just sit with me, understand me and somehow make everything feel okay again. There’s something so soft about tulips, but also something incredibly strong about the way they bloom anyway, and I see that in you. I see it in the way you care, the way you stay, the way you love me without making it feel like a burden. If I ever had to choose a flower that feels the most like “home,” it would be your tulips.

And poppies, my actual favourite. Bright, a little delicate, but full of depth and meaning. I think they represent a part of me that you’ve learned to love so gently. The emotional, slightly chaotic, deeply feeling part of me. You never shy away from it, you never make it feel like too much, you just hold it with care, and that makes me love you even more.

But here’s the thing, if I try to define you with just one flower, it never feels enough because you’re not just a rose or a sunflower or even just tulips. You’re a whole garden. No actually, scratch that, you’re a full-blown flower exhibition in my life. Every kind of love, every shade of warmth, every version of comfort, I find it all in you. You’re the calm and the chaos, the softness and the strength, the quiet and the loud, all blooming together in a way that somehow makes perfect sense to me. And the best part? I never get tired of it. I never stop discovering new things about you, new reasons to love you, new ways you make my world feel fuller. So today, on a day meant for flowers, I just want you to know, you’re the most beautiful thing blooming in my life and I’d choose this garden, you, over and over again.
