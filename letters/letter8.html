My kuchu,

Big question of the day, what is the chemistry behind love (science student hu nap hir)? What is actually happening in my brain, in my body, every time I think about you, every time I hear your voice, every time you say something that makes my heart do that stupid little backflip (I am going to learn to do one myself)?

When you first start liking someone, your brain releases dopamine. That little happiness chemical that makes everything feel exciting, addictive almost. It’s the reason why my mood instantly lifts when I see your name pop up on my phone. Why talking to you never feels enough, like I always want more. It’s not just me being obsessed (hu mai, but that’s not the reason), it’s my brain literally rewarding me for being around you. That makes so much sense, because you are my favourite habit. The kind I don’t want to break, the kind I keep going back to without even realizing it.

Then there’s serotonin, which controls your thoughts. And apparently, when you’re in love, its levels drop, which is why you can’t stop thinking about that person. Because tell me why you’re just there in my head all the time, why I keep replaying our conversations, our moments, our everything, so lets just blame science. Its not even in my control at this point. Its actually embarrassing how much space you take up in my thoughts, but I really wouldn’t have it any other way.

Then comes adrenaline. The racing heart, the slight nervousness, the butterflies. That feeling when you’re about to see me. The way your presence alone can make my heart beat just a little faster (or maybe a lot sometimes). It’s not just emotions, it’s my body reacting to you like you’re something extraordinary, which you are.

But you know what my favourite part is? It’s not just the intense beginning chemicals. It’s what comes after. There’s something called oxytocin, the love hormone. Its released when people feel safe, when they trust each other, when they’re close in a way that isn’t just physical but emotional too and that’s what I feel with you.

A lot of people stop at the dopamine stage. The excitement fades, the rush disappears, and they think the love is gone. But real love, it evolves. Its like we’re a perfectly balanced reaction. Not something explosive that burns out quickly (sodium and potassium metal reaction with air and water), but something stable (gold and platinum), something that lasts. Something that keeps forming and reforming in the best way possible.

And okay, one last nerdy thing before I get too carried away (I hope you like it though). I have said this before I believe, there’s this idea that our bodies are made of atoms that have existed since the beginning of time which meanssss, technically, the atoms in me and the atoms in you, they’ve probably existed together somewhere before. In stars, in the universe, in places we can’t even imagine.

So yes, maybe love is chemistry, maybe it’s hormones and reactions and neurons firing in the right way, but with you, it feels like more than that. It feels like magic that science is still trying to explain.

I love you my favourite chemical reaction ever
