My universe,

The Parallel Universes Theory. You know, the idea that there are infinite versions of reality out there. Infinite versions of you, of me, of us (and of peter parker). Different lives, different choices, different stories. So how much do you wanna bet that we still find each other? Because well I like to believe we do, so I call dibs on betting in favour.

Maybe in one universe, we met much earlier. As kids, sitting in the same classroom, you annoying me by stealing my pencils and me pretending to hate you but secretly waiting for you every day. Maybe we grew up together there, slowly falling in love without even realizing when it started. A perfect friends to lovers rom com.

In another one, maybe we met much later. After becoming completely different people, after living entire lives separately. And still, somehow, one random day, our paths cross and something inside us just clicks. Like even time couldn’t mess it up for us.

Maybe there’s a universe where we never had to do long distance. Where I could just walk over to you whenever I miss you. Where your hugs aren’t something I have to wait for, but something I get whenever I want. That version of us sounds a little less dramatic, but also a little unfair to this one, because I think this distance has made us stronger in ways we don’t even realize.

And okay, maybe there’s a chaotic universe too, where I’m still me (loud, dramatic, slightly annoying) and you’re still you (calm, patient, dealing with me), and we still somehow work perfectly. Because let’s be honest, that dynamic is just inevitable at this point.

But my favourite thought? That in every universe, no matter how different things are, no matter how many variables change, there’s always something that pulls us together. Maybe not the same place, not the same time, not the same version of us. But the same feeling. LOVE.

Because what I feel with you doesn’t seem like something that depends on circumstances. It doesn’t feel fragile or temporary. Its constant. Like it would exist no matter where we were placed in the universe. Like if I didn’t know you yet, I would still be looking for you. And if I found you, I would still choose you. Every. Single. Time.

And maybe in some universe, things are harder. Maybe we don’t get as much time together. Maybe life isn’t as kind. But even there, I don’t think I’d regret loving you. Because even a little bit of you would still feel worth it.

And in this universe? We got it right. Out of all the timelines, all the possibilities, all the versions of reality, we ended up here. Together. Loving each other in the way we do. And I don’t think I’ll ever stop being a little in awe of that. So yes, maybe there are infinite universes out there. Infinite versions of us living infinite lives. But this one? This one is my favourite. Because it has you.

I love you peach, in this universe and in every other
